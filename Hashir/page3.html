Government Schemes To Reduce Poverty

PM Awas Yojana

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) is a major housing scheme launched by the Government of India in 2015 with the aim of providing affordable housing to all. The scheme focuses on helping poor and economically weaker families get a pucca (permanent) house with basic facilities like water, electricity, and sanitation

PMAY has two parts:
PMAY–Urban (PMAY-U) for people living in towns and cities
PMAY–Gramin (PMAY-G) for rural areas


The scheme provides financial assistance, interest subsidies on home loans, and support for building, purchasing, or upgrading houses. Priority is given to women beneficiaries, SC/ST families, economically weaker sections, and people with disabilities.
Overall, PMAY aims to improve living conditions, reduce homelessness, and ensure every family in India has a safe and dignified place to live.

Jan Dhan Yojana

Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY) is a national financial inclusion program launched by the Government of India in 2014. Its main goal is to ensure that every household in the country has access to basic banking services, especially people who never had a bank account before. Under this scheme, individuals can open a zero-balance savings account in any bank. The account includes several benefits like: RuPay debit card Accident insurance cover Life insurance cover Access to direct benefit transfers (DBT) Overdraft facility (after maintaining the account properly) PMJDY has helped millions of people—especially in rural and low-income families—become part of the formal banking system, promoting financial security, savings habits, and transparency in government welfare schemes.

National Food Scurity Act

The National Food Security Act (NFSA), 2013 is a major law by the Government of India aimed at ensuring that every citizen, especially the poor, has access to affordable and adequate food. The Act legally guarantees subsidized food grains to eligible households through the Public Distribution System (PDS).


Under NFSA, about 75% of the rural population and 50% of the urban population are covered. Beneficiary families receive 5 kg of food grains per person per month at highly subsidized prices—₹3/kg for rice, ₹2/kg for wheat, and ₹1/kg for coarse grains.
